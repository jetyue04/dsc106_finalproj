Male Mice: More Stable or Sterotype?

An exploration of mice body temperature

Historically, female subjects have been excluded from medical research due to the belief that male specimens are generally more stable. While this may be true due to the existence in female estrous cycles, is it really true if we account for this phenomenon? Let's take a closer look at this concept in a colony of mice.

Just looking at raw temperatures across all mice, it's not really clear which group is more variable! Let's group together the dataset based on gender, taking the mean of all mice in one gender. Let's also subtract each datapoint by the mean for both groups, shifting means of each curve to 0. We can examine both the variability at each point and the cumulative variance over time.

Temperature Variability
Cumulative Variability

It seems that Male mice tend to have more extreme values than female mice! Looking at the cumulative variance, we can see that the variability of male mice diverges much more quickly than female mice over time.

Female specimens exclusion from research cannot be excused to males possessing less variance. Female specimens tend to show less variability overall.
