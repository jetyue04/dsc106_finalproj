Visualizing Mice Colony

An Exploration of Mice Body Temperature

Mice are a common model organism in biology. They are small, easy to keep, and reproduce quickly. This makes them ideal for studying genetics, behavior, and disease. In this project, we will explore core body temperature and activity levels in a mice. We will use data visualization to better understand the data and draw conclusions from it.

Temperature Variation: Which gender is more stable?

The way that we recorded variance is that we graphed each individual mouse from both the male and female. Then we grouped by gender and subtracted the male data by the male mean, and also the female data by the female male in order for both lines to be around y=0. And with this visualization, we see that the male mice differ a lot more from its respective mean than the female mice.

Write-up:

So far, we have brainstormed various ideas for visualizing the variance in body temperature. We considered using heat maps, histograms, and box plots. However, we decided to stick with line graphs and add animations to them. Our plan is to apply an absolute value to the variance line plot and calculate the area under the curve to determine which variance is higher. This will help us draw conclusions from the data. Additionally, we consulted Professor Ben Smarr, who provided insights from his own research on the topic. He explained his methods and findings, which showed that female mice have less variance in body temperature compared to male mice.

One of the most challenging aspects of this project will be animating the line graph to show the area under the curve over time. We aim to illustrate how the difference between the two genders increases as time progresses. Additionally, we want to present the area under the curve in a clear and comprehensible manner, avoiding the appearance of a typical line plot. Another issue we encountered was selecting an appropriate color scheme for the variance plot. Due to the significant amount of noise, any combination involving blue and another color tended to look cluttered. Thus, another challenge is to ensure the visualization is both aesthetically pleasing and user-friendly. Furthermore, we plan to enhance the interactivity of the first graph. Currently, when a line is clicked, it remains a light color unless hovered over. We intend to modify this behavior so that the line changes to a darker color upon selection, improving the overall user experience.
